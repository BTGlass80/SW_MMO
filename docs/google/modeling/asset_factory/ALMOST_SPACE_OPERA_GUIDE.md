# Almost Space Opera Guide

Date: 2026-07-03  
Purpose: make the "Palworld approach" concrete without turning the project into copied Star Wars assets.

This is not legal advice. It is production guidance for Codex/Claude/humans working on the prototype.

## Short Answer

Yes, we can aim for "almost SW" in the same broad sense that Palworld aims for "monster-catching survival game" energy: close enough that players understand the fantasy, different enough that the assets, names, silhouettes, characters, factions, UI marks, and rules are ours.

The target should be:

```text
space-opera blockcraft MMO
+ WEG-style heroic action, blaster drama, faction pressure, starships, droids, frontier worlds
- exact Star Wars names, logos, helmets, ships, uniforms, species, planets, sound design, or copied silhouettes
```

## Why Palworld Is A Useful But Risky Analogy

Nintendo and The Pokemon Company filed a patent infringement lawsuit against Pocketpair in Japan in September 2024. Nintendo's own release says the suit seeks an injunction and damages over alleged infringement of multiple patent rights. Reporting at the time identified patents related to throwing/aiming capture objects and riding characters, not simply "these creatures look too Pokemon." Later reporting in June 2026 said the claims had reportedly been narrowed to older Palworld versions, likely connected to Pocketpair's preventive gameplay changes.

The lesson is not "copy as close as possible." The lesson is:

- Players respond to familiar genre promises.
- Strong differences in mechanics, presentation, and expression matter.
- Legal risk can still appear even when the claim is not the obvious copyright/trademark claim.
- Preventive design changes are easier before launch than after a lawsuit.

## Green Zone

Use these freely as broad genre language:

- heroic space-opera war stories;
- armored infantry vs. skeletal droid-like enemies;
- frontier desert towns;
- smugglers, bounty hunters, pilots, medics, slicers, scouts, officers;
- blaster-like ranged combat;
- starfighters, freighters, corvettes, carriers, transports;
- faction reputation, duty, obligation, command structure;
- tactical cover, sensors, shields, strain, wounds, skill checks;
- WEG D6-inspired tabletop texture translated into game systems;
- blockcraft / voxel / toy-box art direction.

## Yellow Zone

Use only after making the expression distinct:

- white armored soldiers;
- tan robotic infantry;
- glowing swords or energy melee weapons;
- small dome desert houses;
- cantina-like social hubs;
- bounty terminals;
- hyperspace-like fast travel;
- capital ship corridors;
- wizard/monk/faith traditions;
- large wedge-like warships.

Make them distinct with:

- different proportions;
- different silhouette language;
- different color placement;
- different terminology;
- different faction symbols;
- different planet/culture names;
- different gameplay role framing.

## Red Zone

Do not generate, download, trace, import, or promote:

- exact Star Wars character names or likenesses;
- Jedi, Sith, Clone Trooper, Stormtrooper, Mandalorian, Wookiee, Twi'lek, Tatooine, Coruscant, etc. as in-game production names;
- recognizable ship silhouettes such as X-wing, TIE fighter, Star Destroyer, Millennium Falcon, Slave I, LAAT, ARC-170, Venator, etc.;
- copied helmets, armor panel layouts, weapons, droids, logos, fonts, UI iconography, or insignia;
- ripped/converted assets from official games, films, shows, toys, or fan asset packs without clear rights;
- audio or visual effects meant to imitate the films exactly.

## Naming Pattern

Use generic internal names:

- `white_armored_rifleman`, not `clone_trooper`;
- `tan_battle_droid`, not `battle_droid` if the silhouette is close;
- `frontier_gate`, not `mos_eisley_gate` for production-facing assets;
- `splitnose_fighter`, not `x_wing`;
- `blockade_freighter`, not `corellian_freighter`;
- `faith_knight`, `adept`, `seer`, or `guardian`, not Jedi/Sith.

## Visual Target

The safe target is not "make an X-wing but legally different." The target is:

```text
If Star Wars, Minecraft, Clone Wars tactics, and WEG D6 sat at the same table,
what original blockcraft MMO would they invent?
```

That means:

- chunky readable silhouettes;
- toy-like tactical clarity;
- familiar space-opera roles;
- original factions and markings;
- original ship families;
- original planet names;
- original UI language.

## Asset Review Rule

Every asset should pass this test before promotion:

1. Can a player understand its gameplay role instantly?
2. Does it evoke broad space-opera adventure?
3. Could we describe it without using a protected Star Wars noun?
4. Would a screenshot feel like an original blockcraft MMO rather than a fan remake?
5. Is the source/provenance clean?

If the answer to any of these is no, keep it in `docs/gpt` review only.
